Top 10 Most beautiful butterfly that can
be spotted around the world.

Their name, region lived, family branch and description are all being provided.

10. SYLPHINA ANGEL
Family: Riodinidae Region(s): South America
This butterfly, like the glassing butterfly, has primarily clear leaves with black borders. The tail of the wing is striped in white and crimson. The Sylphina angel can only be found in high-altitude trees and prefers not to move until the sun is scorching.

9. BLUE MORPHO
Family: Morphinae Region(s): Central and South America, Brazil and Mexico
The Blue Morpho is most commonly found on tropical rainforest trees during the combined season. One of the world's largest butterflies, with a wingspan of up to 20 cm, smells through its nostrils (antennae) and hears through its legs.

8. ZEBRA LONGWING BUTTERFLY
Family: Nymphalidae Region(s): America
It is one of the most beautiful butterflies in the world, with long, slim tails and zebra-like stripes on their bodies. The front and back wings have similar forms, although the front has a lighter colour.

7. PEACOCK PANSY
Family: Nymphalidae Region(s): Cambodia and South Asia
It can grow up to 6 cm in length and is commonly seen in gardens and parks. Gorgeous lily eyes on the hind wings distinguish one of the world's most beautiful butterflies, and if you look up the pattern on the wings looks like owl facing.

6. EIGHTY-EIGHT BUTTERFLY
Family: Nymphalidae Region(s): Middle America
The branches' bottoms are red and black. The precise location of the habitat is determined by the colors and luminosity of the pattern on the butterfly's leaves. They prefer to stay close to human settlements and can be spotted in huge groups at times. With a wingspan of about 4 cm, he is a relatively little bug.

5. FOREST GIANT OWL BUTTERFLY
Family: Nymphalidae Region(s): Mexico
This guy, like Owl, has large eyeballs on his wings that resemble owl eyes. This tactic is meant to deter predators because huge degradation's eyes can be seen from afar. Yellow and brown colored marks can be found on the wings. These butterflies can fly a considerable distance and live for 125 to 150 days.

4. EMERALD SWALLOWTAIL
Family: Papilionidae Region(s): Southeast Asia
The arms on the back are red, black, and orange. It's not pigmented, but rather a structural color produced by the butterfly's wing scales, which have an iridescent green spectrum on the sides. The light refracted, and apparent blue and yellow reflections generated an extra green color combination.

3. APOLLO BUTTERFLY
Family: Papilionidae Region(s): Europe
The forewings contain little white spots, while the hind wings have larger red and black dots in a "eyespot" pattern. Adults are terrified by the large places on their bodies and a scentless material, while butterfly larvae are effectively disguised to hide from predators.

2. GRASSWINGED BUTTERFLY
Family: Nymphalidae Region(s): Mexico and Colombia
It eats the Lantana plant, which is a common flowering plant. One of the translucent species is also mentioned on the planet Earth. When it comes to look, the inside and exterior hue of the wings differ drastically.

1. DEAD LEAF BUTTERFLY
Family: Nymphalidae Region(s): Asia
This remarkable butterfly is camouflaged and hidden from predators thanks to its particular system. It has gray, unevenly patterned leaves that resemble a dead leaf in appearance.
