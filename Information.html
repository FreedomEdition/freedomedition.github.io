Lifecycle of Butterflies

A butterfly's life cycle includes a process known as metamorphosis, in which each butterfly passes through four phases from egg to larva, pupa, and finally adult butterfly. Migration

There are a variety of reasons why butterflies migrate to new locations. Did you realize that butterflies are all cold-blooded species? They just cannot cope with the colder temperatures and must go to a warmer location. They must also stay near their food supply; if it is winter and there are no blooms, they will be unable to do so. A Butterfly's Diet

Butterflies are no exception. To survive, they require water. Because they sip the nectar, you observe butterflies fluttering around your flower bed. If you have trees in your yard, there's a good chance you'll see a few butterflies. They like to consume the sap from the trees. This option may catch you off guard. We think of butterflies as beautiful creatures who would never eat something we would consider disgusting. We were mistaken, butterflies will flock to your home to drink the liquid from a pile of rotting animal feces.

Habitat

Mangrove forests, salt marshes, wetlands, lowlands, grasslands, dunes, and mountain zones are among the varied natural habitats that support different butterfly species. Butterfly larvae feed lichens that grow on bare ground and rock surfaces. Some species, such the Elada checkerspot and Empress leilia, may even live in arid environments.
