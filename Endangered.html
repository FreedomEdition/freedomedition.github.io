Causes of population decrease

Let us discover the cause of this decrease.

Habitat loss(Mainly)

Urbanisation is a global phenomenon that has far-reaching implications for wildlife in urban and peri-urban settings. Habitat loss, habitat alteration and degradation, fragmentation, and increased human population and activities all have an impact the butterflies. Habitat loss can occur as a result of housing and infrastructure construction in peri-urban areas, as well as compaction within urban areas.

Milkweed problems

"Caterpillars eat entirely on the leaves of milkweed, the only host plant for this famous butterfly species," according to the NWF. As a result, milkweed is essential for monarch butterfly survival. They won't be able to finish their life cycle without it, and their populations will dwindle." Milkweed contains the toxin cardenolide, which accumulates in caterpillars that feed on it. The cardenolides remain on the caterpillars as adults, protecting them from predators. Birds and predators flee when the poison is detected in the butterflies' colorful wings.

Misuse of pesticide and insecticide

While herbicides have received a lot of attention, butterflies are also vulnerable to pesticides, neonicotinoid insecticides, and fungicides. Non-target pesticides, insecticides, and fungicides, for example, have wreaked havoc on monarch butterflies, according to a Purdue University Department of Entomology study published this summer in Frontiers in Ecology and Evolution.

Climate change

"Butterflies are particularly susceptible to weather and climate," according to the WWF. Reproduction, migration, and hibernation are all triggered by environmental cues (temperature in particular).
